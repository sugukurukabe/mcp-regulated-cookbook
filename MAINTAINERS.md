## Becoming a maintainer

Per [`cookbook-strategy-principles.md`](cookbook-strategy-principles.md) §6.4, the cookbook welcomes additional maintainers. The criteria:

- The candidate has authored or substantively reviewed at least three published chapters.
- The candidate operates an MCP fleet in live operation, ideally in a regulated domain different from the existing maintainers' domain.
- The candidate agrees to the principles in [`cookbook-strategy-principles.md`](cookbook-strategy-principles.md) and the procedures in [`OPERATIONS.md`](OPERATIONS.md).

Interested? Open a discussion under the `maintainership` topic in this repository, or contact a current maintainer directly.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers:

- Review pattern proposals and case study submissions per [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md).
- Apply the procedures in [`OPERATIONS.md`](OPERATIONS.md) when authoring or accepting chapters.
- Update [`gcp-verified-facts.md`](gcp-verified-facts.md) and [`memo-errata.md`](memo-errata.md) as new facts are verified or errors are caught.
- Promote chapters from `draft` to `stable` when field validation criteria are met.
- Participate in [MCP community](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/community) discussions on behalf of the cookbook where relevant.

Maintainers are not expected to:

- Respond instantly. The cookbook operates on a regulated-domain timescale, not a startup-product timescale.
- Accept every proposal. Per [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md), declining a proposal with honest reasons is a normal outcome.
